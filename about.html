ABOUT THE PROJECT

A public archive for private feelings

Unsaid collects anonymous messages that people never sent. The visual language stays editorial, simple, and a little raw, so the words remain the center of attention.

What this is

Unsaid is a place for messages that mattered but were never delivered. Some are apologies, some are love letters, some are just one line that stayed in drafts for years.

Why people use it

The archive gives shape to emotions that are hard to carry alone. Reading what others never said can feel strangely familiar and unexpectedly comforting.

How it works

Write a message, keep it anonymous, add it to the archive, and let it exist without forcing a direct confrontation. The site turns silence into a readable record.

What we protect

We focus on privacy, respectful moderation, and clarity. The project is designed to hold emotional writing without turning it into noise.

"Some messages are not waiting to be sent. They are waiting to be witnessed."
